##Abstract

###Falls are a significant public health issue, particularly among the elderly, where they often lead to severe injuries, long-term disability, and increased healthcare burden. Automatic fall detection systems have become essential for timely medical intervention, yet challenges persist in achieving robust performance without sacrificing usability or privacy. In this paper, we propose a novel fall detection method using wearable sensor data, called LTC-Net (Lyapunov Regularizer-aided Temporal Convolutional Network), \textcolor{blue}{which adopts a fusion-based dual-branch architecture integrating SINDy-based dynamic feature extraction with temporal convolutional modeling.} Our method integrates Sparse Identification of Nonlinear Dynamical Systems (SINDy) coefficients are incorporated as auxiliary features, where SINDy extracts sparse governing ODE coefficients from sensor data via sparse regression, capturing the underlying motion of dynamics. and introduces a Lyapunov based regularization term to enhance model stability and generalization. This architecture effectively learns both spatial dependencies and temporal dynamics via a feature fusion approach. Experimental results on benchmark datasets - including SisFall, KFall, and MobiFall - demonstrate that our model achieves state-of-the-art accuracy while maintaining low false positive rates. Additionally, we have evaluated our method on two sensor-based human activity recognition (HAR) datasets, namely  KU-HAR and USC-HAD, to ensure the robustness of the same. The proposed framework provides a robust and interpretable solution for real-world fall detection applications.
